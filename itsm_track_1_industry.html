🖥️ IT Service — ITSM & ITIL Foundations
What are core industry concepts in IT Service?IT ServicePJF Notebook

ITSM is built around the ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library) framework — the industry standard of best practices for delivering high-quality IT services.

Core Service Lifecycle Concepts

  • Incident: An unplanned interruption to an IT service or reduction in quality (e.g., unable to log in to Zoom).
  • Major Incident: High-impact critical disruption affecting many users. Requires immediate coordinated action.
  • Problem: The underlying root cause of one or more incidents (e.g., faulty authentication server). Involves Root Cause Analysis (RCA).
  • Change (Change Request): Formal request for addition, modification, or removal of anything that could affect IT services. Undergoes Risk Assessment before implementation.
  • Release: A version of one or more configuration items or IT services made available for use, or a formal request to deploy into the live environment.
  • Service Request: A formal, expected user request — VPN access, software installation, new hardware.

Assets, Operations & Metrics

  • Configuration Item (CI): Any physical or virtual component managed to deliver an IT service — servers, network routers, software licenses, cloud services.
  • Service Catalog: Structured list of IT services employees can browse and request.
  • Service Level Agreement (SLA): Formal agreement defining service levels, performance targets, and responsibilities.
  • Knowledge Article: Documented information providing solutions, FAQs, and troubleshooting guidance for self-service and IT staff.
  • Swarming: Collaborative approach where a diverse team of experts rapidly comes together to diagnose and resolve a complex or critical problem.
Give me key industry terms and concepts in ITSMIT ServiceIT Service Notebook

ITSM is the broad practice of designing, delivering, managing, and continually improving IT services. ITIL is the specific playbook — the "how" for structuring high-quality service delivery.

Core Service Management Processes

  • Incident Management: "Wartime" firefighting — restore service fast and minimize downtime.
  • Problem Management: "Fire investigators" — come in after an incident to find root cause and prevent recurrence.
  • Change Management: Structured process for planning, approving, testing, and rolling out updates safely.
  • Release Management: Planning, scheduling, and deploying versions of services into the live environment.
  • Service Request (Request Management): Formal user requests — new software license, laptop, VPN access.
  • Knowledge Management: Capturing and sharing IT information through structured articles to enable self-service and faster support.

Infrastructure & Asset Tracking

  • Configuration Item (CI): Any component — server, router, software license, cloud resource — managed to deliver an IT service. CIs include the asset plus its relationships to other components.
  • CMDB (Configuration Management Database): Dynamic relational repository storing all CIs, attributes, relationships, and dependencies. A giant map of the company's technology and how it's connected.
  • Asset Management (ITAM): Lifecycle management — procurement through retirement — of physical (HAM) and digital (SAM) assets. Unlike a CI (operational relationships), an asset is tracked for financial, contractual, and ownership value.
  • Discovery: Automated process of scanning the IT environment to detect hardware, software, and cloud resources.
How does Problem Management differ from Incident Management?IT ServicePJF Notebook

The primary difference is objective: Incident Management is a short-term reactive response to fix an immediate issue; Problem Management is a proactive long-term process aimed at finding and fixing the root cause so the issue doesn't recur.

Incident Management

  • Definition: Unplanned interruption to an IT service or reduction in quality.
  • Focus: Restore normal service operations as quickly as possible — the "firefighting" mode.
  • Example: Employee reports they can't log in to Zoom.

Problem Management

  • Definition: The underlying or root cause of one or more incidents.
  • Focus: Investigate WHY the disruption occurred. Conduct Root Cause Analysis (RCA) — identify a temporary workaround or permanent solution.
  • Example: After multiple Zoom incidents, IT investigates and determines a faulty authentication server is the root cause.

How They Work Together

The ITSM lifecycle begins with an incident. If similar incidents recur or a complex incident is identified, it triggers a Problem record — moving IT from reactive ticketing to a strategic approach that improves the long-term health and stability of the environment.

What processes, people, and technology are involved in IT service?IT ServiceIT Service Notebook

Processes (ITIL-aligned)

  • Service Operations: Incident Management, Problem Management, Request Management
  • Service Transition: Change Management, Release Management, Configuration Management (CMDB), Knowledge Management
  • IT Asset Management (ITAM): Full lifecycle management of physical hardware and digital software assets

People

  • Requesters (Employees): End-users who submit incidents, browse service catalogs, and seek IT answers
  • Fulfillers (IT Service Agents): IT team members who triage, investigate, and resolve incidents, problems, and change requests
  • Collaborators (SMEs): Specialists pulled into "swarms" to solve complex or major incidents
  • IT Managers: Leaders who monitor metrics, track team performance, and analyze trends
  • Administrators: Technical staff who configure SLA policies and manage the ITSM platform

Technology

  • AI Agents: 24/7 conversational support, instant answers, auto-resolution for routine issues. Co-pilots for IT fulfillers.
  • Omnichannel Interfaces: Slack, Microsoft Teams, Webchat, Email, Employee Portals
  • IT Service Desk Console: Centralized workspace for fulfillers to manage ITIL processes, track SLAs, and view analytics
  • Agentic CMDB & Asset Discovery: Automated tools that scan the network to populate a dynamic CMDB and generate a real-time Service Graph
  • Unified Platform: Data Cloud for secure data sharing across IT, HR, and Customer Service
👥 HR Service — Employee Experience Foundation
Does IT Service include HR needs?IT + HR UnifiedIT Service Notebook

Yes. Salesforce's IT Service approach integrates and supports HR needs as part of a broader, unified employee experience strategy.

  • Combined AI Agents for Employees: The AI Agents for Employees license explicitly provides both "IT and HR service" across the Employee Portal, Slack, Microsoft Teams, and Webchat. A single conversational agent acts as concierge routing requests to the right backend — whether a laptop replacement (IT) or a wellness reimbursement (HR).
  • One-Stop-Shop for Employees: Salesforce views ITSM as a comprehensive Employee Experience Platform, extending beyond IT to encompass HR, Finance, and Facilities.
  • Unified Data Model: IT and HR Services use a Unified Data Model combining IT, HR, Finance, and employee records — a single profile displaying IT incidents alongside HR cases and HR data (title, location).
  • Shared Secure Environment: Because both IT and HR deal with sensitive employee information (HIPAA, payroll, internal IT infrastructure), Salesforce recommends a dedicated employee org, entirely separate from the external Customer Service CRM org.
Does all of ITSM including HR Services use the ITIL framework?IT + HR UnifiedIT Service Notebook

Yes. While ITIL is traditionally a framework for IT teams, Salesforce's unified platform applies the same structured, repeatable methodologies across the entire enterprise — including HR.

  • Core ITIL Compliance: Agentforce IT Service fully supports structured incident management, problem management, change management, release management, service catalogs, approvals, SLAs, and audit trails.
  • Extension to HR Services: Instead of keeping ITIL best practices siloed within IT, Salesforce's unified platform allows HR to utilize the same ITIL-based service management framework for case management, service requests, and approvals — ensuring consistent, trackable service delivery across all departments.

Salesforce takes the proven ITIL playbook used for IT and extends it enterprise-wide so HR and other departments benefit from the same service maturity and automation.

⚔️ Competitive Landscape
What are the biggest pain points for companies running bolt-on chatbots and LLM-based AI?IT ServicePJF Notebook

Key pain points experienced when using competing "AI upstarts" or "bolt-on" conversational AI solutions (such as Moveworks, Aisera, or ServiceNow's legacy AI agents) instead of a natively integrated platform:

  • Increased Complexity and Disparate Tech Stacks: Bolt-on conversational experiences not natively integrated into the company's platform result in a fragmented, disparate tech stack that's hard to manage. Adding tools like Moveworks increases overall complexity.
  • Limited Workflows and Automation: GenAI upstarts often focus only on the front-end employee search experience but have limited overall ITSM offerings. They frequently lack the robust workflow capabilities, APIs, and RPA tools necessary to actually execute tasks.
  • Missing Context and Deep Data Integration: Standalone AI agents often lack deep integration with Knowledge Management and a natively integrated CMDB and Service Graph — required for contextual root-cause resolutions.
  • Struggles with Intent Detection: Legacy AI agents often struggle with accurately detecting user intent — resulting in basic Q&A bots rather than autonomous agents capable of resolving complex issues in the flow of work.
