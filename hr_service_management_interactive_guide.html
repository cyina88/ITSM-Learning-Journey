The Engineering of Human Capital

Modern enterprises don't just manage people; they engineer services. Transitioning from reactive administration to an agile, metric-driven HR Service Management (HRSM) model reduces overhead and elevates the employee experience.

The Structural Design Framework

Before automation, we define the architecture. The "What-Who-How" framework ensures every service is explicitly mapped.

1

WHAT

The explicit catalog of services and resources required for execution.

  • • Service definition
  • • Template compilation
  • • Knowledge base development
2

WHO

The assignment of responsibilities to specific roles and expert skill sets.

  • • Specialist escalation
  • • Generalist triage
  • • Legal & Tax alignment
3

HOW

The digital and physical channels utilized to deliver and measure the service.

  • • Self-service portals
  • • Automated routing
  • • Real-time SLA metrics

The Dave Ulrich model divides HR into four roles along two axes. This matrix ensures that the organization maintains operational discipline while building long-term capability.

HR Business Partners (HRBPs)

Embedded strategic advisors for business units.

Centers of Expertise (COEs)

Subject experts designing global frameworks (Comp, Benefits, L&D).

Shared Services Center

Operational generalists managing high-volume intake.

ER Investigators

Specialists managing sensitive compliance and grievances.

HR IT / IS Specialists

Technical architects of database and workflow integrations.

External Providers

Third-party vendors managing payroll or global benefits.
